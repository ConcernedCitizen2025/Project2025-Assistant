Nathan Leamer

Role: Contributor

Position: Vice President of Public Affairs at Targeted Victory

Background:
Nathan Leamer is the Vice President of Public Affairs at Targeted Victory, a political consulting and communications firm. He has extensive experience in technology policy and public affairs. Before joining Targeted Victory, Leamer served as a Policy Advisor to FCC Chairman Ajit Pai during the Trump administration, where he was involved in major regulatory decisions, including the repeal of net neutrality. Prior to his role at the FCC, Leamer was a Senior Fellow at the R Street Institute, where he focused on emerging technology, intellectual property, and privacy issues. He also has experience working as a legislative aide on Capitol Hill, which provided him with a deep understanding of the legislative process and coalition-building.

Relation to Trump:
Nathan Leamer’s role as Policy Advisor to FCC Chairman Ajit Pai during the Trump administration directly connected him to the administration’s deregulatory agenda, particularly in telecommunications. His work at the FCC involved implementing policies that were aligned with the administration’s priorities, such as reducing regulatory burdens on the telecommunications industry.

Scandals or Controversies:
Nathan Leamer has not been involved in any major personal scandals. However, his work at the FCC, particularly the controversial repeal of net neutrality, has been the subject of significant public and political debate.

Potential Concerns:
Leamer’s involvement in high-profile and often contentious policy decisions, such as the repeal of net neutrality, places him at the center of significant public and industry debates. His role in advocating for these changes can attract both strong support from industry stakeholders and criticism from advocates of stronger regulations.
